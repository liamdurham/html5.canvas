Common Airsoft Rules:

First and Foremost – 

Airsoft is primarily a game of Honor, in that the player who is hit by an airsoft pellet must call themselves "Hit".
While cheating isn't a huge concern in standard play, those who are caught cheating may face severe punishments as a warning to others. All Airsoft games, of all types, revolve around this single, honor-bound rule.

With that said,

Here are a few common rules, reasons, and places you may encounter them:

Rule Reason Field Type
Minimum Engagement Distance To prevent players (typically younger folks) from getting hurt Indoor/Outdoor kid-friendly fields
"Bang Bang" - within x feet To prevent players (typically younger folks) from getting hurt at close distances Indoor/Outdoor kid-friendly fields
No shooting into Spawn To prevent players from being unable to leave thier spawn during games with multiple respawns Indoor fields
Barrel covers/ magazines out To prevent accidental discharges in the staging area All fields
No Incendiary Devices (Smoke grenades) To prevent fire hazards All fields
Full Face-mask Required To prevent damage to teeth or ears Indoor fields
Biodegradable Ammo Only To prevent damage to local wildlife Outdoor fields
